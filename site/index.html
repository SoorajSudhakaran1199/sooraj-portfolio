Master thesis highlight

Industrial robotics with practical execution

The strongest thread in my recent work is the translation of robotics theory into an actual industrial context. My thesis centered on a 6-axis autonomous robot workflow in the KEBA and drag&bot ecosystem, with attention to motion planning, operational constraints, and reliable execution.

I also designed and developed a lightweight web-based joint path planner to enter start and goal joint angles, generate interpolated waypoints, visualize the result, and export CSV files for drag&bot simulation validation.

  • Robot programming for real machine interaction.
  • Motion planning and re-grip logic for practical tasks.
  • Automatic waypoint generation for simulation workflow support.
  • Collision-aware behavior shaped by cell constraints.
  • Integration thinking focused on deployment readiness.
