🏫 School Foundation ＋
1st Standard to 12th Standard

CBSE · Sree Narayana Central School, Kayamkulam

My school journey was completed at Sree Narayana Central School, Kayamkulam, under the CBSE curriculum. This phase built the academic foundation, discipline, and curiosity that later supported my engineering path.

  • Completed 10th standard in 2015 with an outstanding score of 98%.
  • Completed 12th standard in 2017.
  • Built a strong base in mathematics, science, discipline, and structured learning.
🎓 Engineering and Leadership Formation ＋
2017 – 2021

B.Tech Mechanical Engineering · Sree Buddha College of Engineering · APJ Abdul Kalam Technological University

In 2017, I began my mechanical engineering degree at Sree Buddha College of Engineering under APJ Abdul Kalam Technological University. My engineering journey quickly expanded beyond classrooms into leadership, organization building, and project-driven learning.

  • Started as a class representative in the first semester.
  • Played a central role in the development and formation of the Mechanical Engineering Department Association.
  • Served in leadership positions including President and Secretary until the final year.
  • Built strong working relationships with professors, institutional leadership, official members, the Pathanamthitta District Collector, and other public officials.
  • Developed communication, event coordination, leadership, and institutional engagement skills alongside technical education.
Final Year Project · COVID-19 Period

Hospital Service Robot for COVID-19 Support

During the COVID-19 pandemic, I developed a hospital robot project intended to support healthcare-related service tasks. The project gained attention as a timely and meaningful engineering response during a critical public-health period.

  • Developed a hospital service robot during the pandemic period.
  • The project became a notable headline because of its relevance to COVID-19 patient service support.
  • Received recognition from the college as the Best Project of the year.
  • Strengthened my interest in robotics, practical automation, and socially relevant engineering solutions.
🏭 Career Journey and Professional Transition ＋
2021

QA/QC and Oil & Gas Engineering Training

After completing my B.Tech, I pursued focused training in QA/QC and oil & gas engineering for approximately six months. This phase strengthened my knowledge of industrial quality standards, engineering inspection principles, and structured documentation practices.

  • Completed six months of training in QA/QC and oil & gas engineering.
  • Achieved an A+ performance level.
  • Built strong understanding of inspection standards, engineering quality procedures, and industrial workflow discipline.
02/2022 – 09/2022

Non-Destructive Testing Technician · United Engineering and Construction Co., India

This role gave me real industrial field exposure and allowed me to apply inspection knowledge in a practical environment. It became an important bridge between academic engineering and real engineering compliance work.

  • Performed NDT-based quality assessments to support structural integrity and engineering compliance.
  • Documented inspection outcomes in line with engineering and site standards.
  • Developed practical experience in industrial reporting, quality procedures, and technical responsibility.
🌍 The Germany Dream Becomes Real ＋
March 2023

Admitted to THD · M.Eng Mechatronic and Cyber-Physical Systems

My primary focus after mechanical engineering was always to move to Germany for advanced study in mechatronics or a robotics-related field. After a determined preparation process, that goal became reality in March 2023 when I secured admission to Technische Hochschule Deggendorf.

  • Successfully moved toward the long-term goal of studying in Germany.
  • Joined the master’s program in Mechatronic and Cyber-Physical Systems at THD.
  • Expanded from mechanical engineering into robotics, automation, cyber-physical systems, XR, simulation, and intelligent systems.
2025 – Present

Master’s Thesis · KEBA Group, Germany

My academic journey in Germany progressed into direct industrial robotics work through my master’s thesis at KEBA Group. This phase represents the strongest connection between my academic specialization and real industrial automation practice.

  • Working on industrial automation and robotics topics connected to robot programming and deployment workflows.
  • Applying robotics, motion planning, and implementation concepts in an industrial environment.
  • Strengthening the bridge between higher studies in mechatronics and real-world robotics engineering.
2026 – Present

Working Student Role · KEBA Group, Germany

Alongside academic development, I also continued building professional experience through working-student exposure, strengthening my practical engineering readiness within the German work environment.

  • Developed stronger professional adaptation within the German academic and industrial ecosystem.
  • Built applied experience, discipline, and integration into international work culture.
  • Continued aligning academic specialization with long-term robotics and automation career goals.
